Rather than searching for generic interpretations, dreams are treated as a reliable source of data — about who you are, what you carry, and the socio-cultural world you inhabit. Dreams are placed in direct dialogue with waking life through the following approaches:

Activate unconscious and archetypal patterns through active imagination and free association.

Embodied, present-moment engagement with dream figures and images.

Imagery Rehearsal

Somatic & Trauma-Informed
Body-based awareness integrated with trauma-sensitive support throughout all sessions.

About

Camila Figueroa

Camila studied Psychology and later completed a Master's in Global Mental Health in New York City, grounding her work in both clinical rigor and a cross-cultural perspective.

She works at the Mental Wellness Equity Center in the Department of Psychiatry at Columbia University, bringing over a decade of experience translating scientific research into scalable mental health programs, technologies, and real-world interventions.

Questions

What is dreamwork therapy?

Dreamwork offers a way for individuals to engage with unconscious dynamics. It is a practice that uses dreams to help people gain insight into their inner thoughts, process conflicts and tensions, and support personal growth and wellbeing.

How do dreams affect mental health and behavior?

Dreams reflect unconscious processing of emotions, conflicts, and experiences. Research shows that dream content is associated with mood regulation, stress response, and behavioral patterns — making them a valuable and underused source of self-knowledge.

What evidence-based methods does Camila use?

Sessions draw on depth psychology, Gestalt exploration, somatic awareness, and Imagery Rehearsal Therapy (IRT) — all methods with research support for improving emotional clarity and psychological wellbeing.

Who benefits from dreamwork sessions?

Anyone curious about their inner life — whether you're navigating a life transition, experiencing recurring or unsettling dreams, going through relational changes, feeling stuck in a creative project, or simply wanting to understand yourself more deeply. No prior experience with therapy or dreamwork is needed.

What is Imagery Rehearsal Therapy?

IRT is an evidence-based technique originally developed for nightmare disorder. It involves consciously rewriting a distressing dream narrative and rehearsing the new version, reducing nightmare frequency and improving sleep quality.

What languages are sessions available in?
