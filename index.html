The Approach

Not fixed symbols.
Decentralized data
from your inner life.

Rather than searching for generic interpretations, dreams are treated as a reliable source of data — about who you are, what you carry, and the socio-cultural world you inhabit. Sessions bring dreams into direct dialogue with waking life through the following approaches:

I
Depth Psychology
Jungian and psychodynamic frameworks for working with unconscious material and archetypal patterns.
II
Gestalt Exploration
Embodied, present-moment engagement with dream figures, images, and unresolved gestalts.
III
Imagery Rehearsal
Evidence-based technique for reshaping nightmare content and reducing recurrence.
IV
Somatic & Trauma-Informed
Body-based awareness integrated with trauma-sensitive support throughout all sessions.

About

Camila Figueroa

Camila studied Psychology and later completed a Master's in Global Mental Health in New York City, grounding her work in both clinical rigor and a cross-cultural perspective.

She works at the Mental Wellness Equity Center in the Department of Psychiatry at Columbia University, bringing over a decade of experience translating scientific research into scalable mental health programs, technologies, and real-world interventions.

Since 2019, she has developed an integrative, multidisciplinary dream-based practice that helps individuals explore the recurring themes, symbols, and patterns that emerge in dreams. Camila has facilitated more than 150 dreamwork sessions and workshops for people from diverse backgrounds, guiding them in using dreams as practical tools for self-knowledge, emotional clarity, and intentional change.

She is the Head of Science and Innovation at Mindflow, a dream-focused app that helps individuals understand the patterns shaping how they think, feel, and act.
